Abstract

We introduce the Affective Regulation Core (ARC), a control framework inspired by prefrontal cortex functions that maintains stability in agents with internal affective states. Without regulation, such states lead to instability and perseverative loops (a functional analogue to rumination). We also present the Affective Stability & Safety Benchmark (ASSB), a reproducible evaluation protocol with metrics for recovery time, rumination index, and control effort.

Conclusion

We presented ARC, a homeostatic control framework for agents with internal affective states. Our experiments demonstrate:

  • Affective states without regulation lead to collapse (97% vs 30% performance)
  • Meta-control reduces effort while improving stability (-21% ControlEffort)
  • ARC improves RL transfer learning (+50% success in non-stationary envs)
