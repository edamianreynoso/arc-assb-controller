Abstract As AI agents become more sophisticated, they require internal state regulation to avoid instability. We introduce the Affective Regulation Core (ARC), a control framework inspired by the prefrontal cortex. ARC explicitly regulates internal variables like arousal and narrative intensity (a functional proxy for rumination), ensuring agents remain stable even under adversarial perturbations.

1. The Model

We model the agent's internal state as a 10-dimensional dynamical system, decoupling the "feeling" (State) from the "action" (Policy).
